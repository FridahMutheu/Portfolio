What happened, in order
01
Noticed the pattern
While scaling supply to more retail outlets, I tracked production output against the calendar and saw recurring low-output periods that lined up with seasonal local raw-material shortages, not demand.
02
Traced the dependency
Investigated how local material pricing, availability, and quality shifted across the year, and modeled how that cascaded into output, delivery timelines, and vendor commitments.
03
Built the fix, not just the flag
Researched international suppliers in the material's countries of origin, negotiated contracts that fit inside the annual budget, and built in buffer stock for the months local supply typically ran low.
04
Made it stick
Coordinated with operations teams to ensure staffing and process changes were in place year-round, and ran training sessions so the new sourcing rhythm became standard practice, not a one-off patch.
Presented to senior management with full supporting data and signed-ready supplier contracts, so the decision could be approved and acted on immediately.
Stakeholder engagement plan · Illustrative sample

Structuring stakeholder alignment for a multi-country programme

Sample artifact
→
Scenario
A composite scenario modeled on real coordination work: a programme running across multiple field teams, a funder, and an internal product/operations team, each with different priorities and update needs.
Stakeholder map
StakeholderInterestInfluenceEngagement rhythm
Funder / donor Compliance, measurable outcomes, budget adherence High Monthly written report + quarterly review call
Field implementation teams Clear priorities, realistic timelines, fast issue resolution High Weekly check-in + shared live tracker
Internal leadership Risk visibility, milestone progress, resourcing needs Medium Bi-weekly summary, ad hoc escalation as needed
External vendors / contractors Clear scope, timely payment, defined deliverables Low–Med Onboarding brief + milestone-based check-ins
End beneficiaries / community contacts Relevance, trust, follow-through on commitments Medium Field-team-mediated, logged in tracker
→
How this gets used in practice
This map drives the actual cadence: the funder doesn't get pulled into weekly field detail, and field teams don't wait on a monthly cycle to flag a blocker. Each stakeholder gets the right altitude of information at the right frequency, which is what keeps a multi-country programme from drowning in updates or going dark for a month at a time.
